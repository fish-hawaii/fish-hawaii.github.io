## Waikīkī-Diamond Head Shoreline Fisheries Management Area

**Location:** From the 'Ewa wall of the Waikīkī War Memorial Natatorium to the Diamond Head Lighthouse, from the high-water mark out to a minimum seaward distance of 500 yards (or to the seaward edge of the fringing reef if one occurs beyond 500 yards).

### Fishing Periods
- **"Open to fishing"** — January 1 to December 31 of **even-numbered years**
- **"Closed to fishing"** — January 1 to December 31 of **odd-numbered years**

### Permitted
- During "open to fishing" period: fish for, take, or possess any legal-size marine life in season, provided only hook-and-line, thrownet, handnet (to land hooked fish), spear fishing, and hand harvesting methods are used.
- With a permit, engage in activities otherwise prohibited for scientific, propagation, or other purposes.

### Prohibited
- During "closed to fishing" period: fish for, take, or injure any marine life (including eggs), or possess any fishing gear in the water.
- During "open to fishing" period: use any spear between 6:00 pm and 6:00 am; have or possess in the water any trap or net except thrownet or handnet to land hooked fish.
- From one-half hour after sunset to one-half hour before sunrise:
  - Use or possess any spear while diving
  - Possess both diving equipment and a spear at the same time
  - Possess both diving equipment and any specimen of speared aquatic life at the same time

### Transit Through the FMA
Prohibited gear and restricted species may be possessed while onboard a vessel in active transit, provided no prohibited gear is in the water. Vessels adrift, anchored, or moored are not considered to be in active transit (except for vessels in line for the boat ramp and vessels actively loading/unloading at the wharf or shore).

### Scientific Names of Regulated Species
| Common Name | Scientific Name |
|-------------|----------------|
| 'Alakuma | *Carpilius maculatus* |
| Horned helmet | *Cassis cornuta* |
| Triton's trumpet | *Charonia tritonis* |

## Honolulu Harbor

**Location:** Primary port on the southern coast of O'ahu.

### Permitted
- Commercial Marine licensees with a Bait License may take baitfish during periods scheduled by the harbor master.

### Prohibited
- Unlawful to take fish by means of any draw, drag, or seine net.

> **Note:** All boaters must be in contact with the harbor controller (VHF Ch. 12) whenever operating in Honolulu Harbor.

## Maunalua Bay Fisheries Management Area

**Location:** Southeastern coast of O'ahu, from the high-water mark seaward to a straight line extending from the southern tip of Kawaihoa Point (Spitting Caves) westward to the Diamond Head buoy, then a straight line from the buoy to the Diamond Head lighthouse.

### Prohibited
- Take or possess any specimen of the following species: 'alakuma (seven-eleven crab), horned helmet, Triton's trumpet, ula (spiny lobster), or ula pāpapa (slipper lobster).

## Coconut Island — Hawaii Marine Laboratory Refuge

**Location:** Reefs and bay waters surrounding Coconut (Moku-o-Loe) Island in Kāne'ohe Bay, from the high-water mark on the island seaward to twenty-five feet beyond the outer edges of the reefs.

### Prohibited
- Unlawful to take any aquatic life from within the boundaries of the refuge.

### Exceptions
- Does not apply to any officer, faculty member, employee, or student of the University of Hawai'i, or licensee of the Board of Regents, while catching or taking aquatic life for scientific purposes. All authorized taking must follow minimum size and closed season requirements, gear restrictions, etc.

## Ala Wai Canal & Kapālama Canal

**Location:**
- **Ala Wai Canal** — Immediately north of Waikīkī; includes the Manoa-Pālolo drainage canal at the mouth of Manoa and Pālolo Streams.
- **Kapālama Canal** — At the mouth of Kapālama Stream, north of Sand Island.

### Permitted
- Take any legal-size fish in season with one line, or one rod and line, with no more than two hooks.
- Take crabs with not more than 10 nets, provided the nets are not more than two feet in diameter.
- Take shrimp for bait with a hand net, provided the net is not more than three feet in any dimension.
- Within the Ala Wai Canal only: take up to 50 "tabai" or mosquito fish, 'o'opu akupa, tilapia, or a combination thereof per day for non-commercial purposes, with a single small mesh net, provided the net including handle and any attachment is not more than three feet in any dimension.
- Commercial Marine licensees with a Bait License may take baitfishes.
- Licensed pond owners or operators may take young mullet (pua) or other small fish for stocking their fishpond.

### Prohibited
- To fish in or take aquatic life, except as indicated in permitted activities above.

## Wahiawā Public Fishing Area

**Location:** A portion of the privately-owned Wahiawā Reservoir (Lake Wilson) in central O'ahu. The Wahiawā State Freshwater Park (located along the South Fork) includes a boat launching ramp and vehicle-trailer parking. Rules of the State Parks System also apply.

### Permitted
- Fish with hook-and-line with not more than one lure or baited hook or two artificial flies attached to a single line.
  - Weekends and holidays: one line only
  - Weekdays (other than holidays): two lines permitted
- Use attended traps and dip nets up to two feet long, wide, or high (excluding the handle) to land hooked fish or take certain baitfishes, provided that tucunare, bass, channel catfish, and oscar shall not be used as bait.

### Prohibited
- To enter or fish without a valid Freshwater Game Fishing License and entry permit (or other appropriate license/permit), a USCG-approved personal flotation device; all licensees/permittees under 15 must be accompanied by an adult.
- To enter onto, fish from, or approach within 200 feet of the spillway dam and its shoreline, or to enter into or fish within the Forest Reserve along the North Fork.
- To swim, water ski, camp, picnic, build fires, dispose of refuse, or pollute the waters; or damage public or private property.
- To possess any firearms, air rifles or pistols, bows and arrows, spear, trotlines, nets other than dip nets, chemicals, or other illegal fishing gear.
- To snag any fish.
- To operate a boat exceeding 18 feet in length, or for purposes other than fishing, or at speeds in excess of five miles per hour, or constructed of material other than wood unless provided with flotation to render the boat unsinkable.

### Exceptions to Size and Bag Limits
- **Bass and tucunare:** Catch and release fishing only. Unlawful to keep, retain, hold, or kill any bass or tucunare. Any bass or tucunare brought to shore or on board a vessel must be returned to the water immediately.
- **Oscar:** Bag limit 1.

## Nu'uanu Freshwater Fish Refuge

**Location:** City and County of Honolulu's Nu'uanu Reservoirs No. 2, 3, and 4, located between Honolulu and Kailua off the Pali Highway on O'ahu.

### Prohibited
- To enter into the refuge unless accompanied by an agent of, or with a permit issued by, the Board of Land and Natural Resources.

> **Note:** Nu'uanu Reservoir No. 4 is now **closed to fishing**.

## 'Ewa Limu Management Area

**Location:** Waters off 'Ewa Beach on the south shore of O'ahu, from the western edge of the gunnery range to Mu'umu'u Place, from the shoreline 150 feet seaward.

### Permitted
- Hand-pick up to one pound of all types of limu combined per person per day, from 6:00 am to 6:00 pm, during the months of **July, November, and December**.
- Exercise native Hawaiian gathering rights and traditional cultural practices as authorized by law.

### Prohibited
- Pick, gather, harvest, or otherwise take limu except as described above without a permit.
